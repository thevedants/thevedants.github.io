work

My journey in computer science has been shaped by a diverse array of research experiences and innovative projects. Currently, I'm engaged in developing an Automatic Speech Recognition tool for the Bengali language. This project has allowed me to explore the intricacies of language processing and speech recognition technologies, particularly in the context of less-resourced languages.

At Stanford University, I had the opportunity to work on two groundbreaking projects. The first involved developing an efficient Brain-Computer Interface for speech neuroprosthesis, utilizing a transformer-based encoder-decoder model. This experience deepened my understanding of the intersection between neuroscience and AI, showcasing the potential of technology to improve lives. In another project at the Stanford AI Lab, I engineered an autoformalization model for mathematical proofs using foundational models. This work involved fine-tuning Large Language Models (LLMs) to enhance mathematical reasoning, resulting in improved performance on MMLU and Math datasets compared to baselines.

My internship at the MIT Probabilistic Computing Project was another pivotal experience. There, I designed an English to InferenceQL machine translation model. This project not only improved upon pre-existing models but also deepened my expertise in natural language processing and machine translation.

Beyond institutional research, I've led independent projects that have further honed my skills. Energen, a time series-based ML model for efficient electricity management, allowed me to apply machine learning techniques to real-world energy problems. I also authored a research paper on this project, further developing my academic writing skills. Another significant project was LingoGuru, a Sanskrit language learning app that I developed. This project involved implementing a modified edit-distance algorithm and introducing unique gamified features, showcasing my ability to blend technical expertise with user-centric design.

My academic journey has been marked by several honors that have both validated my work and opened new opportunities. Being named a Rise Global Winner was a significant milestone, recognizing my potential to make a global impact. As an RSI Scholar and Mehta Fellow at the Research Science Institute, I received a full-ride scholarship that allowed me to engage with cutting-edge research at an early stage in my career. Additionally, winning a Bronze Medal at the International Olympiad of Linguistics underscored my analytical skills and passion for understanding complex systems, be they linguistic or computational.

These experiences and achievements have collectively shaped my path in AI and computer science. More importantly, they've fueled my passion for using technology to solve real-world problems. As I continue my journey, I'm excited to build upon these experiences and contribute to pushing the boundaries of what's possible in the field of artificial intelligence and computer science. I have recently been intersted in web development and full stack engineering, this website being my first foray in the field.
